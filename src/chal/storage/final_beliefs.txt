# Thesis
- Stance: Grounded in evidence that decisions begin unconsciously (C1), that deterministic neural processes preclude contra-causal choice (C2), that TMS perturbations establish causal necessity of readiness potentials (C3), that meta-analyses reveal no non-physical anomalies (C4), and that neuromodulation interventions demonstrably modulate mental states (C5), we conclude contra-causal free will does not exist.
- Top bullets:
  - Human decision-making is initiated unconsciously by neural processes before awareness (C1).
  - Deterministic neural antecedents negate contra-causal free will (C2).
  - TMS evidence shows readiness potentials are causally necessary for initiating decisions (C3).
  - Meta-analytic reviews find no credible non-physical anomalies, supporting supervenience (C4).
  - Neuromodulation produces reproducible mental changes, providing positive proof of physical supervenience (C5).
- Strength: 0.45
- Reasoning: avg(0.35, 0.35, 0.55, 0.70, 0.75) × (5^1.0 / (5^1.0 + 1)) = 0.54 × 0.83 = 0.45
