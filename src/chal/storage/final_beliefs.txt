# Thesis
- Stance: Empirical neuroscience—spanning cortical readiness potentials (E1, E2) and subcortical intracranial recordings (E5) under physical causal closure (A1)—shows that decisions initiate unconsciously before conscious intention, rendering conscious will epiphenomenal (C1). Conceptual analysis and evidence (A3, E3) confirm quantum randomness lacks agentive control, undercutting libertarian free will (C2). Yet a compatibilist free will—acting in alignment with one’s motivations—remains coherent under determinism and is measurable via sense-of-agency and moral responsibility correlations (E4, E6, C3).
- Top bullets:
  - Decisions are initiated unconsciously in cortical and subcortical circuits, rendering conscious intention epiphenomenal (C1).
  - Quantum indeterminacy does not supply agentive control, undermining libertarian free will (C2).
  - Compatibilist free will—as actions in accordance with motivations—is coherent under determinism and empirically measurable via sense-of-agency and moral responsibility correlations (C3).
- Strength: 0.5591
- Reasoning: avg(0.60, 0.80, 0.60) × (3^1.5 / (3^1.5 + 1)) = 0.67 × 0.84 = 0.5591
