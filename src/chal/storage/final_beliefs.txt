# Thesis
- Stance: Empirical evidence shows that unconscious neural processes initiate decisions before conscious awareness across both simple voluntary actions (C1) and complex moral deliberation tasks (C3). Mind states supervene on brain states within a physically closed system (A1, A3), providing no support for libertarian free will as uncaused agency (C2). Compatibilist reconceptions align with neural causality but do not establish genuine self-determination beyond physical processes.
- Top bullets:
  - Neural precursors systematically precede simple voluntary actions before conscious intent (C1).
  - Unconscious neural predictors extend to complex moral and deliberative decisions (C3).
  - All agent-level choices trace to prior brain states with no evidence for uncaused decisions (C2).
- Strength: 0.5171
