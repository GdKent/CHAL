# Thesis
- Stance: Empirical evidence and the principle of physical causal closure (A1; E1, E2, E4, E5, E6) show that libertarian free will does not exist (C1). At the same time, agents demonstrate compatibilist free will—acting on internal deliberations without coercion (C2)—and this capacity suffices for moral responsibility under determinism (C3). This framework aligns neuroscientific findings with philosophical consensus, preserving real agency and responsibility in a deterministic universe.
- Top bullets:
  - Neural processes determine decisions before conscious awareness, undermining libertarian free will (C1).
  - Agents exhibit compatibilist free will by acting according to internal deliberations without external coercion (C2).
  - Compatibilist free will suffices for moral responsibility, as supported by conceptual foundations and expert consensus (C3).
- Strength: 0.5
- Reasoning: avg(0.60, 0.70, 0.70) × (3^1.0 / (3^1.0 + 1)) = 0.67 × 0.75 = 0.5
