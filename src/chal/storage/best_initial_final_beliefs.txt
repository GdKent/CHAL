## Initial Belief (Stage 1)

# Thesis
- Stance: Empirical evidence rules out libertarian, indeterministic free will (C1) while supporting a compatibilist form—agents respond to reasons in non-coerced decision-making (C2). Given the need for operational definitions (A4), free will exists only in this compatibilist sense, not as undetermined choice (C3).
- Top bullets:
  - Libertarian free will is unsupported: neural precursors consistently precede conscious intent (C1).
  - Compatibilist free will—defined as reasons-responsiveness—is empirically demonstrated (C2).
  - Operational definitions ground meaningful talk of free will, favoring compatibilism over indeterminism (C3).
  - Uncertainties remain about deep indeterministic processes (U1) and moral responsibility scope (U2).
- Strength: 0.54
- Reasoning: avg(0.75, 0.70, 0.70) × (3^1 / (3^1 + 1)) = 0.72 × 0.75 = 0.54

# Definitions
- [D1] **physical determinism**: The doctrine that every physical event, including neural processes, is fully determined by prior states and the laws of physics.
  - Strength: 0.95 (0.95 — well supported by classical and quantum‐scale physical theory; no definitive violations observed.)
  - Used by: A1, A3
  - Status: active
- [D2] **libertarian free will**: The capacity of an agent to make undetermined, indeterministic choices not fully caused by prior physical states.
  - Strength: 0.85 (0.85 — standard philosophical concept; coherence debated but widely used in literature.)
  - Used by: A3, A4
  - Status: active
- [D3] **compatibilist free will**: A capacity for agents to act according to their desires and reasons without external coercion, even if those actions are determined.
  - Strength: 0.85 (0.85 — widely adopted in philosophy as an operational notion of freedom under determinism.)
  - Used by: A4, A5, E2
  - Status: active
- [D4] **readiness potential**: A measurable neural signal that reliably precedes and predicts the timing of an intended voluntary movement.
  - Strength: 0.9 (0.90 — replicated across EEG and related methods since Libet (1983).)
  - Used by: A2, E1
  - Status: active
- [D5] **reasons-responsiveness**: The property of decision-making systems to modulate actions in response to deliberated reasons.
  - Strength: 0.85 (0.85 — core component of compatibilist definitions; empirically studied in psychology.)
  - Used by: A5, E2
  - Status: active
- [D6] **conscious awareness**: Subjective recognition of a mental event or intention in real time.
  - Strength: 0.9 (0.90 — phenomenologically accessible and operationalized in neuroscience.)
  - Used by: A2, E1
  - Status: active
