# Thesis
- Stance: Empirical evidence and the principle of physical causal closure (A1) support that libertarian free will (D2) does not exist (C1), as neural processes determine decisions before conscious awareness. However, agents exhibit compatibilist free will (D3): the capacity to act on internal deliberations without coercion (C2), which aligns with behavioral data (E3) and retains practical moral responsibility in deterministic systems.
- Top bullets:
  - Libertarian free will lacks empirical support; decisions are preceded by deterministic neural events (C1).
  - Compatibilist free will exists as an operational capacity for motivation-based action without coercion (C2).
  - Physical causal closure and neural determinism underpin the rejection of contra-causal freedom (A1, E1, E2).
  - Behavioral studies confirm agents act on internal deliberations, satisfying compatibilist criteria (A2, E3).
  - Compatibilist free will provides a scientifically tractable framework for agency and responsibility.
- Strength: 0.4833
- Reasoning: avg(0.75, 0.7) × (2^1/(2^1+1)) = 0.725 × (2/3) = 0.4833
