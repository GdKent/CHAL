# Thesis
- Stance: Grounded in robust empirical findings of unconscious decision initiation (C1) and a clear contra-causal definition of free will (A3), there is no support for agents having the capacity to choose otherwise under identical conditions. While compatibilist accounts may preserve a pragmatic notion of agency, our analysis shows that contra-causal free will does not exist in human agents (C2).
- Top bullets:
  - Decision-making begins unconsciously via neural processes before conscious awareness (C1).
  - Contra-causal free will, defined as the ability to choose otherwise under identical conditions, lacks empirical support (C2).
  - Readiness potentials and fMRI predictions consistently predate conscious reports (E1, E2).
  - Our analysis is scoped to contra-causal free will, excluding compatibilist notions by design (A3).
  - Empirical methods provide a reliable basis for these conclusions (A2).
- Strength: 0.47
- Reasoning: avg(0.70, 0.70) × (2^1/(2^1+1)) = 0.70 × (2/3) ≈ 0.467
