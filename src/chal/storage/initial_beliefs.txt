# Thesis
- Stance: The existence of free will is a complex question with significant empirical challenges. Empirical evidence suggests that our decisions are influenced by a variety of factors including genetics, environment, and neurological processes. While some level of apparent choice exists, the extent to which this constitutes 'free will' is uncertain. Current scientific understanding does not conclusively support the existence of free will as traditionally conceived.
- Top bullets:
  - Empirical data suggests decisions are influenced by external and internal factors.
  - The concept of free will is not clearly supported by current neurological evidence.
  - Free will, as traditionally understood, lacks measurable empirical support.
- Confidence: 0.4
