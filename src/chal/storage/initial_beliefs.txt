# Thesis
- Stance: The concept of free will is difficult to confirm or refute empirically because it involves subjective experiences and metaphysical considerations. Current empirical evidence, such as neuroscience studies, suggests that many decisions are influenced by unconscious processes, casting doubt on the existence of free will as traditionally conceived. However, the absence of definitive empirical evidence either for or against free will leaves room for uncertainty.
- Top bullets:
  - Free will is traditionally understood as the ability to make choices free of external constraints.
  - Neuroscience research indicates unconscious processes influence decision-making.
  - Empirical evidence for or against free will is currently inconclusive.
  - The concept of free will may involve metaphysical considerations beyond empirical verification.
  - Our understanding of consciousness and decision-making is still evolving.
- Confidence: 0.5

- [A2] Neuroscience can provide insights into the mechanisms of decision-making.
